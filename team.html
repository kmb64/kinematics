Pep Karalus

Founding Director and Musculoskeletal Biomechanist

Pep has been working as a musculoskeletal sports clinician for the past 8 years. Pep specialises in the assessment of whole body biomechanics with a special interest in lower limb musculoskeletal injuries.

Pep started out his career studying Physiotherapy before transitioning on to do Podiatry in order to gain a more extensive understanding of the ground reaction forces of the foot and the result of these forces on the rest of the body. After completing Podiatry, Pep furthered his biomechanical knowledge by completing multiple running and performance courses.

Throughout his career, Pep has worked for provincial, national and international sporting teams and was a provider for the New Zealand Rugby Union for 5 years. Pep has also worked closely with players from the Otago ITM and Super 15 Rugby teams as well as representative athletics, basketball, cricket and netball players.

Pep assesses the biomechanics and alignment of the body as a whole and does not just treat an area in isolation. Treatment techniques include; foot, ankle and knee mobilisations, dry needling, remedial massage, Prolotherapy and exercise rehabilitation. Pep works closely alongside Ken Craig, the Vice President of the International Society of Medical Shockwave Treatment and founder of Isokinetics and Shockwave Health and Performance Clinic.

Pep is a national distributor of KenesioLogic taping and in the near future looks forward to running biomechanics courses incorporating education on the correct application of this groundbreaking product.

Joshua Ioane

Partner and Musculoskeletal Physiotherapist

Josh has over 5 years experience working as a Musculoskeletal Physiotherapist in New Zealand and has a special interest in sporting injuries and post-operative rehabilitation.

Josh incorporates his passion for biomechanics into the assessment and treatment of soft tissue and joint injuries. By identifying movement dysfunctions, Josh can diagnose the primary root cause of the injury and therefore provide patients with a specific treatment plan with a long-term outcome.

Joanna Daves

Joanna has been a dedicated musculoskeletal physiotherapist since 2009 and has treated a vast array of injuries and conditions during this time. She is also trained as a clinical pilates instructor and has a special interest in treating pelvic and spinal injuries.

Over the years she has continued to expand her expertise in the area of biomechanics to determine the reason behind a persons pain to treat the cause, rather than the symptoms. Joanna enjoys seeing her patients through all stages of their rehabilitation.

Joanna has also loved working with a sporting demographic both in clinic and at the Hyundai A-League and Westfield W-League for the Football Federation of Australia as the treating physiotherapist for the match referees since 2010.
